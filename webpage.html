// Why this matters

Motivation in P3

Thermal stress is one of the leading causes of reduced growth rates, illness, and mortality in commercial pig farming. Manual monitoring is reactive and inconsistent — especially across barns with mixed-age herds.

🌡️

Age-Dependent Comfort

Piglets, young pigs, and adults have fundamentally different thermal needs. A single thermostat cannot serve a mixed herd — the P3 system profiles each animal group and finds the least-stress compromise.

🤖

Autonomous Response

Rather than rule-based triggers, the RL agent learns nuanced control — it can anticipate sustained cold snaps, respond to metabolic heat from adult pigs, and distinguish thermal stress from illness-induced stress.

📡

Sensor-Ready Deployment

The model was designed around real hardware constraints: binary on/off equipment, temperature-only sensors, and no ambient weather feed. It works with what a barn actually has, not what a lab assumes.

// Model design

What the Agent Observes

The PPO agent receives an 11-dimensional observation vector every timestep and outputs 4 binary actions — one for each heater and fan.

Dim Feature Description
0air_tempCurrent barn air temperature (°C)
1perceived_tempBiological felt temperature (lagged)
2deltaAir minus perceived — rate of change signal
3–4heater_1, heater_2Current heater states (0 or 1)
5–6fan_1, fan_2Current fan states (0 or 1)
7–9count_0/1/2Number of piglets, young, adult pigs
10total_stressCombined thermal + non-thermal stress signal

// Stress decomposition

Thermal vs. Sick Pig Stress

A key design feature is the separation of controllable thermal stress from non-thermal illness stress. The agent learns that when non-thermal stress is high, temperature adjustments alone won't solve it — requiring a separate intervention module.

Cold Adults — No Illness

All observed stress is thermal and fixable by the agent

Result: Thermal stress quickly drops to near zero as the agent activates heaters. Total stress tracks perfectly with thermal stress — there is nothing else going on.

Cold Adults — With Illness Event

Non-thermal stress spike at step 100 despite stable temperature

Result: Thermal stress is resolved quickly. At step 100, total stress spikes due to illness — the agent correctly holds optimal temperature rather than overreacting, correctly deferring to the external vet/alert module.
// Development journey

Training Pipeline

The model was built through 5 progressive stages, each addressing a failure mode discovered in the previous evaluation.

01

Base PPO

10-dim env, basic reward shaping. Heaters and fans on MultiDiscrete action space.

200k steps
02

Cold Fix

3× cold penalty, biased resets toward cold starts and adult/young herds to fix freezing behaviour.

+100k steps
03

Robust (11-dim)

Added total_observed_stress dimension. Introduced random sick pig events during training.

+200k steps
04

Metabolic

Age-dependent metabolic heat output. Adults generate 6× more heat than piglets per step.

+200k steps
05

Diurnal

Full 24h sinusoidal ambient temperature cycle. Final evaluation across 5 real-world scenarios.
