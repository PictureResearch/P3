Pig Behaviour Classification

An automated pipeline that uses deep learning and heuristic rules to detect and classify pig behaviours from overhead barn video as part of the P3 Precision Pig Farming project.

Why This Model Is Needed

The P3 (Precision Pig Production) project aims to improve pig welfare and farm efficiency using continuous, non-invasive monitoring of behaviour. Manual video scoring is slow, costly, and often inconsistent.

Key Motivations

  • Early health detection: Changes in movement, posture, or feeding can indicate illness or stress before visible symptoms appear.
  • Welfare compliance: Automated records provide consistent and auditable evidence for monitoring and reporting.
  • Scalable monitoring: A single camera can track all pigs continuously without observer fatigue.
  • Data-driven management: Behaviour data helps optimize feeding, pen layouts, and environmental controls.
  • Research value: High-resolution data supports studies in precision livestock farming and animal behaviour.
  • Cost efficiency: Automating video analysis reduces labour and resource needs.

The system combines a ResNet-18 classifier with motion- and zone-based heuristics for fast, reliable behaviour classification from overhead video, enabling practical on-farm deployment.

Model Overview

The pipeline has two complementary components that work together to classify pig behaviour frame-by-frame:

1. ResNet-18 for Lying vs Standing Classifier

A pre-trained ResNet-18 (ImageNet weights) is fine-tuned on cropped pig images from annotated video sequences. The final fully-connected layer is replaced with a 2-class output (lying / standing). Training uses 5-fold cross-validation on a balanced dataset of 1,500 images per class, augmented with flips, rotations, colour jitter, and random crops.

2. Heuristic Rule Engine

Before the CNN is even consulted, a set of spatial and motion rules classifies the more distinctive behaviours:

RuleConditionAssigned Behaviour
Feeder zone overlap > 10 % & velocity < 10 pxPig's bounding box overlaps the feeder regionEat
Drinker zone overlap > 10 % & velocity < 7 pxPig's bounding box overlaps a drinker regionDrink
Velocity < 2 pxNearly no movementLying
Velocity 2 – 5 pxSlight movementStanding
Velocity 15 – 20 pxModerate movementWalk
Velocity > 20 pxFast movementRun
OtherwiseAmbiguous velocity (5 – 15 px)CNN predicts Lying / Standing

The CNN acts as a fallback for frames where motion alone is ambiguous, ensuring robust classification even when pigs are shifting weight or slowly repositioning.

Zone Detection via Background Mask

A manually created background mask (GIMP) highlights the feeder and two drinker areas. OpenCV contour detection identifies the three largest blobs and extracts their bounding boxes, which are then used at inference time to test bounding-box overlap with each zone.

Behaviour Scenarios

The model classifies each detected pig into one of six behaviours. After evaluation, walk, run are merged into moving and sleep is merged into lying for 5-class reporting.

Lying / Sleep

Pig is stationary with very low velocity (< 2 px between frames). The CNN also distinguishes lying posture from standing when velocity is ambiguous.

Standing

Pig is upright with slight movement (velocity 2 – 5 px). In ambiguous cases the ResNet crop classifier makes the final call.

Eating

Pig's bounding box overlaps the Feeder Zone by > 10 % while velocity is low (< 10 px) — indicating stationary feeding behaviour.

Drinking

Bounding box overlaps either drinker zone (left or right) by > 10 % with velocity < 7 px — the pig is at the nipple drinker.

Walking

Moderate frame-to-frame displacement (velocity 15 – 20 px). Merged into the moving class for final 5-class evaluation.

Running

High displacement (> 20 px). Also merged into moving. Typically occurs during play or startle events.

Step-by-step detail

  1. Zone detection — reads a hand-made background mask, finds the three largest contours (feeder, left drinker, right drinker) via OpenCV, and stores their bounding-box coordinates.
  2. Crop extraction — iterates over training sequences, reads output.json annotations, decodes corresponding video frames, and saves cropped pig images into lying/ and standing/ folders.
  3. Data balancing — augments the minority class with random flips, rotations, colour jitter and crops until both classes reach 1500 samples.
  4. ResNet-18 training — 5-fold cross-validation on the balanced dataset; saves the best-fold checkpoint (.pt file).
  5. Validation inference — for each validation sequence:
    • Interpolates bounding boxes between annotated keyframes.
    • Computes per-frame velocity.
    • Applies the heuristic decision tree (eat → drink → lying/standing/walk/run).
    • Falls back to the CNN for ambiguous velocity frames.
  6. Evaluation — computes overall accuracy and generates both a full and a 5-class confusion matrix.

Key code snippet — heuristic decision tree

# Per-frame behaviour decision (simplified)
if overlap_feeder > 0.1 and velocity < 10:
    behaviour = "eat"
elif overlap_drinker > 0.1 and velocity < 7:
    behaviour = "drink"
elif velocity < 2:
    behaviour = "lying"
elif 2 <= velocity <= 5:
    behaviour = "standing"
elif velocity > 20:
    behaviour = "run"
elif 15 < velocity <= 20:
    behaviour = "walk"
else:
    # Ambiguous — ask the CNN
    behaviour = resnet18_predict(crop)  # "lying" or "standing"
